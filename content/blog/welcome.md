Every journey has a first step. On the chai trail, it's usually a clay cup pressed into your hands before you've even had the chance to sit down.

That's the spirit behind **Chairasta** — a path you walk with a cup in hand, unhurried.

## What this blog is about

This is a place for everything chai:

- **Recipes** — from a simple ginger-cardamom brew to complex masala blends
- **Ritual** — the act of brewing, the patience it demands, the calm it delivers
- **Stories** — how chai travels from a Himalayan hillside to a Tehran bazaar to a London flat

## A simple recipe to start

Here's the chai that started it all for me:

**Ingredients (1 cup)**
- 1 cup water
- ½ cup whole milk
- 1½ tsp loose leaf black tea (Assam or Darjeeling)
- 2 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 1 thin slice of fresh ginger
- Sugar to taste

**Method**

Bring water to a boil with the cardamom and ginger. Add the tea, reduce heat, and simmer for 2 minutes. Add the milk and bring back to a gentle boil. Strain and sweeten.

Sit down. Hold the cup with both hands. Drink slowly.

That's Chairasta.
